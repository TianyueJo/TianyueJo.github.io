Research Experience

Undergraduate Research Assistant, Prof. Cathy Wu, MIT LIDS · Cambridge, MA

Online Preference Learning for Drivers

Sept 2023 – Jan 2024

Objective: Implement online learning algorithms for travel route recommendation to rapidly capture user preferences and recommend personalized, eco-friendly routes.

  • Developed Expert with Clustering (EWC), a contextual bandit framework leveraging hierarchical user information, achieving a 27.57% improvement over LinUCB.
  • Proposed Loss-guided Distance, a metric tailored for online preference learning that improves centroid representativeness in clustering.
  • Established regret bounds for EWC with superior theoretical performance compared to LinUCB.

Modeling User Non-compliance in Recommendation Systems

Jan 2024 – June 2025

Objective: Address user non-compliance in physical-world recommendation problems where users can dismiss recommendations and revert to baseline behavior.

  • Introduced the Nah Bandit framework for modeling user non-compliance in recommendation systems.
  • Proposed a user non-compliance model to parameterize the anchoring effect, reducing bias in preference learning.
  • Combined the non-compliance model with EWC, achieving at least 15.36% improvement over supervised learning and traditional bandits across multiple applications.

Structure Detection for Contextual Reinforcement Learning

Aug 2024 – Aug 2025

Objective: Extend model-based transfer learning to multi-dimensional contextual reinforcement learning (cRL) problems.

  • Proposed SD-MBTL, a framework that detects generalization structures and adapts task selection strategies accordingly.
  • Developed M/GP-MBTL, combining clustering and Gaussian Process approaches for different structural patterns in CMDPs.
  • Validated on robotics, eco-driving, and agriculture benchmarks with significant gains in generalization and sample efficiency.
